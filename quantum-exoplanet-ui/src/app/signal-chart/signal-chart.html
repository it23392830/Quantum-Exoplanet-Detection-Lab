HOW THIS WORKS

This tool helps scientists search for planets orbiting distant stars — thousands of light-years away — without ever seeing those planets directly.

① Watch a Star's Brightness
We point a telescope at a distant star and record how bright it appears over time. Under normal conditions, the brightness stays roughly constant.
② Spot the Dip
When a planet passes in front of its star (called a transit), it blocks a tiny fraction of the starlight. This causes a brief, faint dip in brightness — like a fly walking across a light bulb.
③ Analyse the Signal
The chart below shows that brightness over time — the light curve. A noticeable dip in the middle of the graph is the key signature of a planet passing by.
④ AI Makes the Call
A machine-learning model analyses the shape of the dip and calculates a confidence score — how likely it is that a real planet caused it, rather than random noise or another star.
